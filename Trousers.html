A Beginner's Guide To: Trousers

Everyone is human, we all put our trousers on leg at a time, but finding the exact right pair can be the challenge.
Here, we will do our best to get you started by trying to find at least three pairs of trousers to get your basic wardrobe started up.

Grey Flannels

"The only pair of trousers a guy needs are grey flannel."

-"Nick Wooster"

Worn by men throughout time, grey flannel trousers are a surefire bet to strike the balance for anywhere from smart casual to even formal occasions.
Be sure to opt for a tapered cut, and avoid pleats at all costs!
Grey flannel trousers are a wardrobe essential, being able to be incorporated into wildly differing aesthetics from rugged workwear or Americana and even Ivy or the more insufferably foppish stylings of the #menswear subculture.

Indigo Raw Denim

Unwashed, unsanforized "raw" denim is a far more personal experience, allowing your jeans to break in to your body and create an almost custom fit.
Opt for a dark wash indigo or black, and make sure to wash them as rarely as possible to ensure stark contrast of fades once worn in.
For a good starting point, go for an Unbranded UB101.
If you're willing to go for a more expensive entry point, opt for 3Sixteen's ST-120x.

Khaki Chinos

The workhorse of any business casual wardrobe, a good pair of chinos is essential to any wardrobe.
For a more trad fit, aim for a tapered leg, but a full rise to sit at the natural waist and more flattering proportions.
While this fit is hard to find in the modern market, J. Crew's Essential Chino gives a fuller rise while still having a modern silouhette.
