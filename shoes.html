A Beginner's Guide To: Footwear

White Sneakers

A compliment to every outfit, a simple pair of clean white sneakers can make any outfit pop and break up a monochromatic color scheme or even add a stark finish for somehting loud and colorful.
While there are many options from budget to premium, as long as you opt for a minimalist silhouette and avoid maximalist stylings in trend right now (save the triple S's for the hardcore enthusiasts), you are off to a great start! Below are a list of options and the pricepoints ordered in ascending order (bolded options are the "editor's choice"):

  • Vans Authentic Canvas-$45.00
  • Adidas Stan Smith-$75.00
  • Nike Killshot 2-$75.00
  • Nike Air Force 1 Low-$100.00
  • Common Projects Achilles Low-$423.00
  • Maison Margiela Relicca German Army Trainers-$470.00

While it may be intimidating to look at the higher price tiers of the list, don't worry! Entry level picks like the Stan Smith may very well be the best and serve as the inspiration for higher end products like the CP Achilles low!

Casual Boots

For more business casual occasions, a casual brown boot is the way to go. Signaling to an Americana aesthetic, these boots add a workwear tinge to the most drab and sterile of all office jobs.
With everything from chukka boots all the way to actual workboots, these options will be able to slot in easily to any business casual wardrobe. (bolded options are the "editor's choice")

  • Clark's Original Desert Boot-$80.00
  • Chippewa Apache Service Boot 6"-$205.00
  • Red Wing Iron Rangers-$305.00
  • Wolverine 1000 Mile Work Boot-$365.00

Black Suede Sneaker

In this case, there is only one way to go: Vans Old Skools in a black colorway. The low profile of the shoe allows it to fit with any color scheme, as well as provide a slim silhouette to work with any pants from straight cut to full on skinny.
Opt for the low-top old skools over the Sk8-hi's for their versalility in four season wear over the F/W limitied Sk8-hi's.
